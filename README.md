## Final Project: Traffic Crash Patterns and Injury Severity Analysis in Boston: Multilayer Perceptron and Bayesian Modeling
### Traffic crashes remain a major public safety concern in cities like Boston, where road conditions, traffic, and environmental factors all play a role in determining not only when crashes occur but also how severe they might be. In this project, we examine crash data from Boston to explore how certain conditions increase the severity of crashes. To identify safety trends, specifically what features can support predicting the severity of traffic accidents, we utilized a multilayer perceptron (MLP) neural network and Bayesian modeling. The dataset contains additional information about the event that could increase the severity of crashes, such as type of vehicle, road surface condition, ambient light, weather condition, and whether they occurred at intersections or not. By combining these two approaches, we aim to gain an understanding of when crashes happen, as well as what conditions make these crashes more dangerous. This combination of both analyses helps distinguish our work from research that only explores one side of crash data.
